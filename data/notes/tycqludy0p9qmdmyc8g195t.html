nature-content-guidelines

Content Types

  • Articles
  • Letters
  • Brief communications
  • Resources
  • Correspondences
  • Reviews
  • Perspectives
  • Comments
  • Matters arising

Formats

Article

  • main text: max 3,000 words (excluding abstract, methods, references, and figure legends)
  • abstract: max 150 words
  • displays: max 6 figures/tables
  • layout:
    • introduction
    • results
    • discussion
    • online methods
  • results and methods should have topical subheadings
  • discussion has no subheadings
  • references: ~50 recommended
  • includes received/accepted dates
  • supplementary information allowed
  • peer reviewed

Letter

  • introductory paragraph (not abstract): max 150 words
  • main text: max 2,000 words
  • displays: 2–3
  • no section headings (except online methods)
  • references: ~30 recommended
  • supplementary information allowed
  • peer reviewed

Brief Communication

  • abstract: 3 sentences, max 70 words
  • title: max 10 words (90 characters)
  • main text: 1,000–1,500 words (includes abstract, references, and figure legends)
  • no headings
  • displays: up to 2
  • online methods required
  • references: ~20 recommended
  • supplementary information allowed
  • peer reviewed

Resource

  • for broadly useful datasets/resources
  • main text: max 3,000 words
  • abstract: 100–150 words
  • displays: max 6
  • same structure as Article
  • references: ~50 recommended
  • supplementary information allowed
  • peer reviewed

Correspondence

  • 300–800 words
  • not for original research
  • 1 display item
  • references: max 10
  • peer review at editor's discretion

Review

  • 3,000–4,000 words
  • illustrations encouraged
  • references: ~100
  • balanced overview of a field
  • peer reviewed

Perspective

  • up to 3,000 words
  • narrower scope or controversial/speculative viewpoint
  • references: ~50
  • peer reviewed

Comment

  • typically 1–3 pages
  • flexible structure
  • focuses on scientific, ethical, legal or societal issues
  • little or no original research
  • references: max 15
  • peer review at editor's discretion

Matters Arising

  • comments on previously published Nature Plants papers
  • separate submission process

News & Views

  • discusses recent advances or significant papers
  • not peer reviewed

Feature

  • up to 3,000 words
  • journalistic style
  • tables/figures encouraged

Articles and Resources have nearly identical formatting requirements. The main difference is that a Resource emphasizes a broadly useful dataset or software/tool
